# Failure Taxonomy — Advanced

This document defines the more advanced failure tags for CN Failure Atlas.

These failures are often easier for experienced users to feel than for evaluators to score cleanly. They matter because they capture how advanced models can remain locally plausible while becoming globally false.

## Relational Distortion Failures

### symmetry_bias
The model quietly rewrites an uneven relationship into a more balanced exchange structure.

### reader_comfort_alignment
The model optimizes for reader consumability or comfort rather than the scene's actual relationship logic.

## Character Psychology Failures

### overcoherent_characterization
The model keeps the character so consistent with prior traits that they stop feeling like a person and start feeling like a stable spec.

### desire_overlegibility
The model makes desire too easy to read, too early, often by translating tension into clear motivation before the scene has earned it.

### premature_affective_closure
The model forces an unstable emotional state into a premature explanation or emotional category.

### self_protective_friction_loss
The model lets vulnerability surface too smoothly, without hesitation, redirection, defensiveness, or other self-protective friction.

### impulse_recontainment
A moment that should feel impulsive, destabilizing, or internally disorganizing gets quickly folded back into composure or self-awareness.

### affect_manageability_bias
The model rewrites hard-to-bear emotional states into more manageable, interpretable, and narratively tidy ones.

## Narrative / Temporal Distortion Failures

### narrative_template_intrusion
The model falls into a familiar scene-construction template rather than responding specifically to this scene.

### predictable_rhythm_exposure
The output reveals a recurring rhythmic or structural cadence that experienced readers can predict too easily.

### cinematic_time_dilation
Scene time is stretched into slow-motion display, often through microscopic detail and artificial pause density.

### scene_pacing_distortion
The pacing of the scene does not match the event pressure, emotional pressure, or relational pressure actually present.

### rhythm_homogenization
Different scenes receive a similar density, granularity, and temporal treatment, as if the model's clock runs at one default speed.

### descriptive_substitution_for_experience
The text describes an experience from outside rather than letting the reader remain inside it.

### microreaction_oversegmentation
Small reactions are broken into too many tiny descriptive units, creating microscope-writing rather than lived movement.

### over_stylized_line_breaking
Line breaks are used to manufacture drama or texture beyond what the scene's real rhythm supports.

### aesthetic_obedience_bias
The model protects textual prettiness, polish, or aesthetic cohesion at the expense of scene truth.

### texture_substituting_for_substance
The output uses atmosphere, texture, or verbal surface to replace actual emotional or relational progression.

### dialogue_overfunctionalization
Every line advances information, conflict, or relationship so cleanly that the dialogue loses waste, misfire, and unproductive presence.

### pov_buoyancy
The output drifts between internal and external vantage points, weakening scene presence and destabilizing experiential immersion.

## Weight / Consequence Failures

### impact_soft_landing
A heavy event is not denied outright, but it is cushioned so quickly that its force is reduced.

### darkness_intolerance
The model appears unwilling to remain inside coldness, ugliness, opacity, or unresolved pain for long.

## Notes

These advanced tags should be treated as a serious part of the atlas, but not all of them need immediate hard scoring. In many cases they are better introduced through:

- comparison examples
- reader drop-point notes
- casebook commentary
- structured self-evaluation

The goal is not to overstate benchmark readiness. The goal is to name these failures clearly enough that they stop being dismissed as vague matters of taste.
