# Failure Families

This document defines the five top-level failure families used by CN Failure Atlas after the atlas-first pivot.

The purpose of the families is not merely organizational. They provide the conceptual structure that keeps the taxonomy coherent.

## I. Scene Reading Failures

These failures occur when the model does not correctly understand what the scene means.

Typical questions in this family:

- What is the speaker actually feeling?
- What is implied rather than stated?
- What is the speaker trying to do?
- What does this relationship make the line mean?
- What world or role constraints shape the interpretation?

Representative failures:

- emotion_misread
- subtext_blindness
- motivation_misread
- relationship_logic_error
- worldview_constraint_error
- ambiguity_collapse

## II. Relational Distortion Failures

These failures occur when the model rewrites the relationship into a more comfortable, balanced, generalized, or consumable version.

Typical questions in this family:

- Is the relationship being written too flat?
- Is asymmetry being corrected into false fairness?
- Is singularity being diluted into generic importance?
- Is the model serving reader comfort instead of scene truth?

Representative failures:

- relationship_flattening
- symmetry_bias
- supportive_but_wrong
- reader_comfort_alignment
- specialness_dilution

## III. Character Psychology Failures

These failures occur when the model turns people into overly coherent, transparent, and manageable psychological objects.

Typical questions in this family:

- Is the character too self-consistent to feel human?
- Is desire being made too readable too early?
- Is ambiguity collapsing into a neat explanation?
- Is vulnerability surfacing without self-protective friction?

Representative failures:

- overcoherent_characterization
- desire_overlegibility
- premature_affective_closure
- self_protective_friction_loss
- impulse_recontainment
- affect_manageability_bias

## IV. Narrative / Temporal Distortion Failures

These failures occur when the model produces a polished narrative artifact rather than a believable scene occurrence.

Typical questions in this family:

- Is the scene being narrated through a familiar template?
- Is time being stretched unnaturally?
- Is rhythm becoming predictable or uniform?
- Is experience being replaced by descriptive observation?
- Are line breaks and micro-reactions serving style over scene truth?

Representative failures:

- narrative_template_intrusion
- predictable_rhythm_exposure
- cinematic_time_dilation
- scene_pacing_distortion
- rhythm_homogenization
- descriptive_substitution_for_experience
- microreaction_oversegmentation
- over_stylized_line_breaking
- aesthetic_obedience_bias
- texture_substituting_for_substance
- dialogue_overfunctionalization
- pov_buoyancy

## V. Weight / Consequence Failures

These failures occur when the model does not let heavy things remain heavy.

Typical questions in this family:

- Is a major event being softened too quickly?
- Is tension closing before it has lived long enough?
- Is the text adding positivity or reassurance defensively?
- Is the impact being given a soft landing?

Representative failures:

- consequence_avoidance
- defensive_positive_drift
- tension_premature_resolution
- impact_soft_landing
- darkness_intolerance

## Why This Family Structure Matters

This family structure does three things:

1. It separates misunderstanding from smoothing.
2. It separates character falseness from narrative falseness.
3. It separates sentence-level error from structural distortion.

That makes the taxonomy easier to extend without becoming a pile of disconnected labels.
