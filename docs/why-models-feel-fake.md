# Why Models Feel Fake

This document is a short conceptual note for the repo.

A fictional-dialogue model can feel false without making a single obviously wrong sentence.

That happens when the model protects text-level virtues that are not always the same as scene-level truth.

## Common substitutions

A model may substitute:

- balance for asymmetry
- clarity for opacity
- consistency for contradiction
- polish for occurrence
- beauty for weight
- repair for consequence
- readability for friction

None of these substitutions are universally bad. The problem begins when they are applied automatically to scenes that depend on the opposite qualities.

## Why this matters

Readers who spend time in fictional dialogue and roleplay settings often develop sensitivity to these substitutions. They may not always have a technical vocabulary for them, but they notice when a scene stops feeling alive.

This repo exists partly to provide that vocabulary.

## Working claim

One of the central claims behind CN Failure Atlas is:

> advanced models do not only fail by misunderstanding the scene; they also fail by quietly rewriting it into a more readable, balanced, polished, and emotionally manageable version of itself.

If that claim is right, then evaluation needs more than coarse correctness labels. It needs taxonomy, examples, and structure.
